IELTS General Reading

📖 Complete Practice Guide

Master all three sections of the IELTS General Reading test with our comprehensive practice materials. From social survival contexts to workplace communications and general interest articles, each section is designed to simulate authentic exam conditions with detailed explanations and strategic guidance.

📋 Test Overview

⏰ Total Duration

60 minutes (including transfer time)

Key Information

  • 40 questions total across 3 sections with variable question distribution
  • Total text length 2,150-2,750 words
  • Three sections of increasing difficulty from social survival to general interest
  • Section 1: 2-3 short texts or several shorter texts (everyday topics)
  • Section 2: 2 texts (workplace contexts)
  • Section 3: 1 long text (general interest)
  • 11 different question types from multiple choice to diagram completion
  • Transfer answers to answer sheet within the 60-minute time limit
  • Band scoring from 1-9 in whole or half bands (e.g., 6.5, 7.0)

🏠 Section 1: Social Survival Context

Question Range & Time

Questions 1-14 (typically 12-14 questions) | ~20 minutes

Section 1 may contain 2-3 short texts or several shorter texts based on social survival in English-speaking countries. These texts are factual, informative, and non-argumentative, focusing on practical everyday situations such as accommodation, transport, shopping, and leisure activities.

Common Text Types

  • Advertisements: Job ads, housing rentals, product promotions, services
  • Public notices: Announcements, timetables, warnings, regulations
  • Brochures & leaflets: Tourist information, course descriptions, facility guides
  • Labels & signs: Instructions, directions, safety information
  • Forms: Application forms, registration documents, booking forms

Question Formats & Typical Ranges:

📝 Multiple Choice Q 1-4 or Q 8-11
Choose 1 correct answer from 3-4 options (A, B, C, D). Tests understanding of specific factual information from advertisements, notices, or simple texts.
✓ True/False/Not Given Q 5-8 or Q 1-6
Assess if statements agree with factual information in the text. Common in notices, advertisements, and informational texts.
📋 Form/Note Completion Q 9-14 or Q 7-12
Fill gaps in application forms, booking forms, or note summaries. Word limits apply (e.g., "NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS").
🔗 Matching Information Q 10-14 or Q 4-8
Match specific details (prices, times, features) to different options or text sections. Common with multiple advertisements or facilities.
✏️ Short Answer Questions Q 1-5 or Q 11-14
Answer factual questions about names, dates, prices, times, or locations using words from the text.
📄 Sentence Completion Q 6-10 or Q 8-14
Complete sentences with information from notices, advertisements, or informational texts.

💼 Section 2: Workplace Context

Question Range & Time

Questions 15-27 (typically 12-14 questions) | ~20 minutes

Section 2 contains 2 texts related to work contexts in English-speaking countries. These texts are more complex than Section 1 and deal with work-related issues, training, professional development, workplace policies, and employment matters.

Common Text Types

  • Job descriptions: Detailed role requirements, responsibilities, qualifications
  • Company policies: Workplace rules, procedures, guidelines, benefits
  • Training materials: Course content, professional development programs
  • Staff handbooks: Employee guidelines, procedures, workplace information
  • Work communications: Memos, emails, reports, meeting minutes

Question Formats & Typical Ranges:

🏷️ Matching Headings Q 15-20 or Q 18-23
Match paragraph headings (i, ii, iii) to sections of workplace policies, training materials, or job descriptions.
📝 Multiple Choice (Complex) Q 21-25 or Q 15-19
More complex multiple choice questions requiring inference about workplace procedures, policies, or requirements.
🔗 Matching Features Q 22-27 or Q 19-24
Match job requirements to descriptions, training modules to outcomes, or workplace benefits to categories.
📊 Table/Summary Completion Q 16-22 or Q 20-27
Complete tables with workplace information, training schedules, or policy summaries using words from the text.
⚖️ True/False/Not Given Q 18-25 or Q 15-21
Verify factual accuracy of statements about workplace policies, procedures, or job requirements.
📑 Matching Information Q 23-27 or Q 17-22
Find specific details in different paragraphs of work documents, policies, or training materials.

📰 Section 3: General Interest

Question Range & Time

Questions 28-40 (typically 12-16 questions) | ~20 minutes

Section 3 contains one longer, more complex text on a topic of general interest. This text is more descriptive and instructive, taken from newspapers, magazines, books, and online resources written for a non-specialist audience. The style is generally more sophisticated than Sections 1 and 2.

Common Text Types

  • Newspaper articles: Current events, social issues, lifestyle topics, health
  • Magazine features: Science, technology, travel, culture, environment
  • Book extracts: Non-fiction, biographical content, instructional material
  • Research reports: Survey findings, factual investigations, studies
  • Online articles: Educational content, how-to guides, general interest topics

Question Formats & Typical Ranges:

👁️ Yes/No/Not Given Q 28-33 or Q 30-35
Identify writer's opinions, views, and claims rather than facts. Tests understanding of the author's perspective and attitudes.
📝 Multiple Choice (Advanced) Q 34-37 or Q 28-32
Complex multiple choice requiring inference, interpretation, and understanding of implicit meaning in academic-level texts.
🔚 Matching Sentence Endings Q 31-36 or Q 35-40
Complete sentence beginnings with logical endings, testing understanding of complex ideas and their relationships.
📋 Summary Completion Q 32-40 or Q 28-34
Fill gaps in article summaries, testing comprehension of main ideas and supporting details in complex texts.
📊 Flow-chart Completion Q 36-40 or Q 30-36
Complete process descriptions, scientific procedures, or step-by-step explanations from complex texts.
🏷️ Matching Headings Q 28-35 or Q 32-38
Match headings to paragraphs in complex articles, identifying main themes and ideas in academic-level content.
📐 Diagram Labelling Q 37-40 or Q 33-40
Complete labels on scientific, technical, or process diagrams based on detailed descriptions in the text.

💡 Study Tips & Strategies

Before the Test

  • Practice reading different text types: advertisements, workplace documents, articles
  • Familiarize yourself with all 11 question types and their specific requirements
  • Build vocabulary for everyday life, workplace, and general interest topics
  • Practice time management: aim for 20 minutes per section
  • Learn to skim for main ideas and scan for specific information efficiently

During the Test

  • Read questions carefully before reading the text to focus your attention
  • Underline key words in questions to guide your reading
  • Don't spend too long on difficult questions - move on and return later
  • Transfer answers carefully - spelling and grammar must be correct
  • Use only information from the passage, not your general knowledge
  • For True/False/Not Given, distinguish between facts and opinions

Section-Specific Strategies

  • Section 1: Focus on factual details like names, numbers, dates, and times
  • Section 2: Pay attention to workplace terminology and procedural language
  • Section 3: Look for writer's opinions and understand complex arguments
  • All sections: Follow question number order - answers appear sequentially

Key Success Factors

  • Regular practice with authentic English materials from various sources
  • Understanding of different text purposes and organizational patterns
  • Strong vocabulary across social, workplace, and academic contexts
  • Effective exam techniques for each question type
  • Ability to work efficiently under time pressure
